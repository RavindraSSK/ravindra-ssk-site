Records that hold emotional weight

Short reflections on albums that carry a memory, a season, or a way of thinking that sticks around after the last track.

Albums as timestamps

Some records are tied to a city, a commute, or a phase of work. Replaying them is less about novelty and more about returning to a known emotional frame.

What I write down

  • Where I first heard it properly (headphones, car, late-night desk)
  • What problem I was working on at the time
  • One lyric or motif that still surfaces in memory

Why keep notes at all

A personal listening archive makes music part of the same creative record as writing and photography. It is a map of moods, not a ranking.
